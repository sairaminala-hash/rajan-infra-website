How Much Does It Cost to Build a House in Hyderabad?

Building a house in Hyderabad in 2026 can range from ₹3 Lakhs for small renovations to ₹50+ Lakhs for luxury villas. The biggest factors are land area, number of floors, materials used, and finishing quality.

Cost Breakdown by Category

  • Budget Construction: ₹1,200 – ₹1,800 per sq ft. Basic materials, standard fittings. Ideal for 2BHK independent houses. Total for 1000 sqft: ₹12-18 Lakhs
  • Modern Construction: ₹2,500 – ₹3,500 per sq ft. Good quality cement (Ultratech/ACC), branded tiles, modular kitchen. Total for 1000 sqft: ₹25-35 Lakhs
  • Luxury Construction: ₹4,500 – ₹6,000+ per sq ft. Italian marble, premium wood, smart home wiring, designer interiors. Total for 1000 sqft: ₹45-60+ Lakhs

Key Cost Factors

  • Foundation: 10-15% of total cost. Depends on soil type (black cotton soil in Hyderabad needs deeper foundations)
  • Structure (RCC): 25-30%. Cement, steel, sand, aggregate
  • Brickwork & Plastering: 10-12%. Red bricks vs AAC blocks
  • Electrical & Plumbing: 8-10%. Concealed wiring, CPVC pipes
  • Flooring: 8-10%. Vitrified tiles (₹40/sqft) to Italian marble (₹300+/sqft)
  • Painting: 5-7%. Asian Paints, Berger, or Nerolac
  • Doors & Windows: 5-8%. UPVC windows, teak wood doors
  • Interiors: 10-15%. Kitchen, wardrobes, false ceiling

Best Areas to Build in Hyderabad

Popular areas include Madhapur, Gachibowli, Kompally, Bachupally, Narsingi, Shamshabad, and Financial District. Land costs vary from ₹3,000/sq yard in outskirts to ₹50,000+ in prime locations.

Tips to Save Money

  • Buy cement and steel in bulk during off-season (June-August)
  • Choose AAC blocks over red bricks — faster construction, better insulation
  • Use gypsum plastering instead of sand plastering — smoother finish, less paint needed
  • Go for UPVC windows — no maintenance, no painting, lasts 25+ years

When Should You Renovate?

  • House is less than 20 years old with good structural health
  • You want to update kitchen, bathrooms, or flooring only
  • The foundation and columns are in solid condition
  • Budget is between ₹3-10 Lakhs

When Should You Reconstruct?

  • House is 25+ years old with major cracks in walls/columns
  • Water seepage in foundation or walls
  • You want to add extra floors
  • Old electrical wiring and plumbing is unsafe

Renovation Costs in Hyderabad (2026)

  • Painting (Full House): ₹15-25 per sq ft. A 2BHK costs ₹30,000-60,000
  • Kitchen Renovation: ₹1.5-4 Lakhs (modular kitchen with granite top)
  • Bathroom Renovation: ₹80,000-2 Lakhs per bathroom
  • Waterproofing: ₹30-50 per sq ft for terrace/bathroom
  • Flooring Replacement: ₹50-150 per sq ft including labor
  • False Ceiling: ₹60-120 per sq ft (gypsum/POP)
  • Complete Renovation: ₹800-1,500 per sq ft

Pro Tips for Renovation

  • Always waterproof before re-tiling bathrooms and terrace
  • Replace old GI pipes with CPVC/PPR pipes — lasts 50+ years
  • Use putty + primer before painting for a smooth finish
  • Choose POP/gypsum false ceiling over wood for termite-free life

Best Cement Brands for House Construction

  • UltraTech Cement: Most popular in Hyderabad. Great for RCC and general construction. ₹350-400 per bag
  • ACC Cement: Strong and reliable, excellent for foundations. ₹340-380 per bag
  • Ambuja Cement: Known for durability, good for high-rise. ₹350-390 per bag
  • Zuari Cement: Local favorite in Telangana, cost-effective. ₹310-350 per bag
  • Penna Cement: AP/Telangana brand, great for budget builds. ₹290-340 per bag

Best Paints for Indian Weather

  • Asian Paints Apex Ultima (Exterior): Best weather resistance, 7-year warranty. ₹300-400/litre
  • Berger WeatherCoat (Exterior): Excellent anti-fungal properties. ₹280-350/litre
  • Asian Paints Royale (Interior): Premium washable, smooth finish. ₹350-500/litre
  • Nerolac Excel (Budget): Good quality at lower cost. ₹200-280/litre

Steel (TMT Bars)

  • Tata Tiscon: Premium quality, highest strength. ₹55,000-60,000/ton
  • SAIL TMT: Government standard, very reliable. ₹50,000-55,000/ton
  • Vizag Steel (RINL): Local AP brand, excellent value. ₹48,000-53,000/ton

UPVC vs Aluminum vs Wooden Windows

  • UPVC: Best value. No painting, no rusting, soundproof. ₹350-500/sqft. Lasts 25+ years
  • Aluminum: Lightweight, modern look. ₹250-450/sqft. Lasts 15-20 years
  • Wooden: Traditional look, needs maintenance. ₹600-1500/sqft. Termite risk

Money-Saving Material Tips

  • Buy TMT steel when rates drop (check mandi rates weekly)
  • Use M-Sand instead of river sand — cheaper, consistent quality
  • Choose 43-grade cement for plastering, 53-grade for RCC
  • For painting, always use putty + primer — saves 20% paint
